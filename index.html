Numerical simulations for compressible multi-components flows involve many difficults. On the one hand, high resolution schemes are needed to resolve delicate flow structures such as Kelvin-Helmholtz instabilities and cell-like structures in supersonic flow. On the another hand, most high resolution schemes may fail to capture sharp discontinuous solutions robustly. Thus, high-fidelity solvers, which are accurate and robust, are highly demanded. At the same time, the concept of high performance computing (HPC) will be implemented with GPU.

-Shock Water Interaction

A right moving Mach 6 shock in the air interacts with a water cylinder. The simulation may fail with some high resolution schemes such as WENO because density becomes negative. The present result is calculated by proposed MUSCL-THINC-BVD.

-High Resolution Schemes

As benchmark tests, double Mach reflection and Mach 3 wind tunnel tests are used to show the high resolution property of the developed solver.

-3D Aircraft Simulation

The schemes are having been extended to unstructured grids. 3D aircraft is simulated with proposed VPM-BVD scheme.

-3D Shock Helium Bubble Interaction

The current 3D simulation is conducted with single GPU. In future, the solver will be extended to multi-GPU to realize high performance computing.

-Stiff Detonation Waves

The developed solver show promising results to solve stiff detnoation waves. WENO scheme can not resolve correct detonation front due to the lack of enough spatial resolutions. However, the proposed MUSCL-THINC-BVD scheme can capture correct position.
